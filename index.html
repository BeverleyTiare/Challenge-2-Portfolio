About

My work as the Director of Definition Diverse involves research development as well as collaboration with UCLA PEERS in professional training for health professionals and educators across Australia and NZ.

We have partnered with Universities, Schools, Charities, Neurodiversity Employment Organisations and Health Organisations, including "Headspace, Orygen, and Child Adolescent Mental Health Services(CAMHS)", as well as a collaboration with the award-winning "Northern Pictures" for Netflix’s “Love on the Spectrum”.

My focus is upon delivery of evidence-based and positive psychology approaches to enhance outcomes for individuals, teams and organisations by engaging with systemic change. I have post-graduate qualifications in Coaching Psychology, Cognitive Science and Developmental Neuroscience and I am a qualified Yoga Teacher. Outside of work I love family time, yoga, art and design.

Future Directions

A new type of neuroscience is emerging: within the myriad ranks of theorists have risen teams of neuroscientists that do science with data on neural activity. These evolving teams are not creating methods for analysing data nor gathering data - these neuroscientists are implementing the full gamut of modern computational techniques on that data to answer scientific questions about the brain - A neural data science has now emerged.

But, we don’t know how our brains encode behaviour - so we work with behavioural labels, but we almost always know these are not the full answer. They are just clues to possibilities.

Systems neuroscience is an expansive playground for those who can fuse their knowledge of neuroscience to their know-how for analysing data. Neural data science is now in our midst.

Lets delve a bit further...a neural data scientist asks scientific questions about data from systems neuroscience to reveal how neurons work together to do what they do. Now, there are ways we can answer that question - by looking at established classes of problems in machine learning and computational challenges in systems neuroscience.
